Education

Elman Peace offers a range of education programs, including a Girls' Leadership Academy, adult non-formal education, primary education, infrastructure development and building schools in rural Somalia, teachers education and curriculum building, and leadership and peacebuilding training.

Sports for development

A program that utilizes sports as a tool for conflict prevention, peace building, and community development for young people in Somalia.

Human rights

Our Human Rights program focuses on defending victims of human rights abuses and provides legal aid, while also advocating for policy change and raising awareness on a global level through platforms like the Universal Periodic Review and UN Security Council.

Employment

A program that prepares and supports men and women in Somalia to pursue dignified livelihoods by providing marketable skills and connecting them to employers who recognize and value talent and merit over ethnicity, nepotism, gender or physical ability.

Employment

Sister Somalia provides life-saving support to survivors of sexual and gender-based violence through one-stop crisis centers, offering psychosocial counseling, trauma healing, and emergency medical care, while equipping survivors with skills to lead self-determined lives and prevent further violence within their communities."

Peace By Africa

Peace by Africa is a civil society network that supports inclusive reintegration and community-based peace building strategies in areas of protracted conflict by connecting organizations with field experts and sharing best practices.

Our Story

Elman Peace is named after its founder, Elman Ali Ahmed;  an ardent and pioneering peace activist, remembered to this day as the Somali Father of Peace. Elman and his wife, Fartuun Adan ran the organization together until the war worsened in Somalia, and they made the decision to split up;

Elman would stay behind and continue to fight for peace and Fartuun would flee the country for the safety and future of their daughters. It would be the last time Fartuun and her children would see Elman alive.

Elman Ali Ahmed

an ardent and pioneering peace activist, the Somali Father of Peace.

Read more

A few years later Elman was the victim of a political, execution style assassination on March 9th 1996.

“His murder has sent a chilling message to Somalis desirous of peace and normality.” - Amnesty International

FARTUUN ADAN

A passionate humanitarian, educator & leader

Fartuun Adan is a passionate humanitarian, educator and leader. She fled Somalia in the early 1990s with her 3 young daughters in tow, leaving her husband and partner to her humanitarian endeavours behind, never to see him alive again.
