Education

We believe education can be a critical deterrent to violence and that violence can be a critical deterrent to education.

Our belief is that by interceding through education in the critical period of adolescence, we can help to unlock tremendous leadership potential and foster a whole new life trajectory for the next generation of youth in Somalia

Girls Leadership academy:

We provide innovative, life-saving support to those in need and strive to create more enabling and progressive environments through our work. Our philosophy on aid and development is centered on locally driven solutions from a committed and professional collective of compassionate people.

The leadership academy was built in honor of Almas Elman; an ardent girls education activist whose life was taken too soon in Mogadishu, Somalia.

The leadership academy provides free education to 200 girls for 4 years so that they too may become fearless, compassionate leaders who can champion the rights of other women and girls in Somalia and around the world; like her ….

Adult Non-Formal Education:

We know girls and women fare worst in times of conflict and extreme poverty when accessing education and those that from the internally displaced people’s communities face even more barriers. It is for that reason, our non-formal education and second-chance literacy programs for vulnerable communities provide free classes for displaced women and out of school youth in conflict with the law.

These non-formal education programs focus on excluded groups including pastoralists and people in remote rural communities, families displaced by conflict or drought and ethnic minorities.

Primary Education:

Our formal education programs for children are designed with the long-term goal of building academic, social and emotional knowledge that will prepare children to fully participate in their societies. Our primary & middle schools provide numeracy, literacy and recreational activities in impassioned environments that foster a positive learning experience for children.

We know that children are most motivated to learn when they are having fun and feel safe; it is for that reason we apply the Montessori educational pedagogy to our learning environments, allowing each child to learn at their own rhythm.

Infrastructure Development & Building Schools in Rural Somalia:

Building schools during war-times is important for reviving the educational process that can facilitate a peaceful transition out of conflict. With education still 100% privatized in Somalia, school restoration is a vital step towards democratizing access to learning for all.

To respond to this, we build, equip and refurbish schools across Somalia in order to create access to free education to the most vulnerable members of society.

Teachers Education & Curriculum Building:

We develop rights-based curricula that builds the capacity of our teachers and empowers them to develop creative lesson plans that keep their students engaged and inspired.

For education to galvanize the societal progress we know it can, we start with the education of our educators.

Leadership & Peacebuilding Training:

In the face of the many complex challenges of modern conflict, which see education far down the list of priorities; we emphasize the role that education coupled with peace building can play in mitigating the worst effects of conflict.

To respond to this, we build, equip and refurbish schools across Somalia in order to create access to free education to the most vulnerable members of society.
